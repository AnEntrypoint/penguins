🐧 Galapagos Penguin

Spheniscus mendiculus

Endangered
← Back to all species
Scientific NameSpheniscus mendiculus
Height48-53 cm
Weight1.7-2.6 kg
Population1,200
RangeGalapagos Islands, Ecuador (primarily Fernandina and Isabela)
HabitatRocky lava coastlines and cool upwelling zones
DietSardines, mullet, small schooling fish
Breeding SeasonVariable; triggered by cool water temperatures
Clutch Size2 eggs
Lifespan15-20 years

⚠ Threats

  • El Niño events drastically reducing food availability
  • Introduced predators (cats, rats, dogs)
  • Oil spills from fishing vessels
Fun Fact:

The Galapagos penguin is the only penguin species that regularly lives and breeds north of the equator.

Data sourced from IUCN Red List, BirdLife International, and Antarctic Treaty resources.
