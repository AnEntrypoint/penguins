Facts about Gentoo Penguin

behavior

Gentoo penguins are the fastest penguin swimmers, reaching speeds of up to 36 km/h. They are also known to porpoise — leaping in and out of the water like dolphins — which reduces drag and allows faster travel.

Recent Research

  • Stokes et al. (2024): Investigated Gentoo penguin foraging behavior using bio-logging devices, revealing adaptive dive strategies in response to shifting prey distributions around the Antarctic Peninsula.
  • Black & Team (2023): Documented the northward expansion of Gentoo penguin breeding colonies into previously unoccupied sub-Antarctic islands, correlating with regional climate warming trends.
  • Petry et al. (2022): Analyzed stable isotope signatures in Gentoo penguin feathers, demonstrating dietary plasticity and increased reliance on fish over krill in warming sub-Antarctic regions.
