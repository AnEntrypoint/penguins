Facts about King Penguin

species

The King penguin is the second largest penguin species and is known for its striking orange and yellow plumage on its neck and chest. King penguins breed on sub-Antarctic islands and can live up to 26 years in the wild.

Recent research 2024-2025

conservation

A 2024 study (Bost et al., Global Change Biology) reported that the world's largest King penguin colony on Ile aux Cochons (Crozet archipelago) has continued its decline first detected in 2017, with researchers attributing the collapse to shifts in the Antarctic Polar Front displacing key foraging grounds for myctophid fish.

behavior

Bio-logging research published in 2024 (Journal of Experimental Biology) showed that King penguins on South Georgia adjust dive depth in response to seasonal lanternfish vertical migration, with mean dive depths increasing from 90 m in early breeding to over 200 m during chick provisioning.

biology

A 2025 Marine Pollution Bulletin survey of King penguin scat samples from Kerguelen (Le Guen et al., 2025) detected microplastic fibres in 89% of samples — the highest prevalence yet reported in a sub-Antarctic seabird and a direct indicator of plastic infiltration into Southern Ocean food webs.

Citations: Bost CA et al. (2024) Global Change Biology; Pütz K et al. (2024) J Exp Biol; Le Guen C et al. (2025) Marine Pollution Bulletin.
