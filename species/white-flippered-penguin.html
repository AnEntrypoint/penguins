🐧 White-flippered Penguin

Eudyptula albosignata

Vulnerable

Scientific NameEudyptula albosignata
Height30-40 cm
Weight1-1.5 kg
Population4,000
RangeBanks Peninsula and Motunau Island, Canterbury, New Zealand
HabitatRocky coastal areas with burrows in limestone cliffs and boulders
DietSmall schooling fish, squid, krill
Breeding SeasonAugust to January
Clutch Size2 eggs
Lifespan6-20 years

⚠ Threats

  • Introduced predators (ferrets, cats, dogs)
  • Coastal development and habitat loss
  • Marine pollution and entanglement
Fun Fact:

The white-flippered penguin is distinguished by the broad white margins on both edges of its flippers, unlike the plain dark flippers of its closest relative.
